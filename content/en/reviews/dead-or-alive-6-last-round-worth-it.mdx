## Gameplay Mechanics: The Classic Triangle System

Dead or Alive has always set itself apart from other 3D fighters through its intuitive yet deep rock-paper-scissors mechanic, known as the Triangle System. Understanding this system is crucial to appreciating the game's competitive depth.

*   **Strikes beat Throws**: Fast punches and kicks interrupt grab attempts.
*   **Throws beat Holds**: If an opponent tries to counter your strike with a hold, you can execute a high-damage throw.
*   **Holds beat Strikes**: Anticipating an opponent's strike allows you to catch their limb and perform a devastating counter-hold.

This system creates a psychological battle of reads and counters. The addition of the Break Gauge in the sixth installment adds a layer of modern resource management, allowing players to perform a Break Blow (a powerful cinematic strike) or a Break Hold (a universal counter that works against any strike type).

| Mechanic | Input Type | Direct Counter Target | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strikes** | Punch / Kick Buttons | Throws | Medium (Vulnerable to Holds) |
| **Throws** | Grab Button | Holds / Guards | High (Vulnerable to Strikes) |
| **Holds** | Back + Guard + Strike | Strikes | High (Vulnerable to Throws if missed) |
| **Break Blow** | Forward + Special | All standard attacks | Low (Requires full Break Gauge) |
